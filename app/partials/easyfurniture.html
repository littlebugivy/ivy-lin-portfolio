Problem Description

Buying a set of brand new furniture is not cost-effective particularly for students who regard the place as a temporary residency (i.e. short-term study, potentially move to better/cheaper places). Besides, dealing with furniture after finishing a short-term stay is also a headache, as it causes a huge waste to throw away furniture in good condition, or another bill to move them around.

Easy Furniture aims at connecting local second-hand furniture sellers and buyers in a more efficient way.

Concepts Forming

Research on competitors (Competitive Analysis)

After investigating a number of used-furniture trading platforms both online and offline, I concluded four models of operation.

Traditional Trading

Examples: Local market

Sellers bring used-item to a physical place, buyers go, investigate and then take home.


Investigate in-person, chance for price negotiation
Inflexibility in time and space

Information Synthesis Platform

Examples: Craglist, AABBS, online forums

Sellers post information of used-item online, leave their contact details and then the buyer get in touch privately. The online platform only synthesize information.


Chance for price negotiation
Concerns on information reliability, personal information leak

Online Shopping Platform

Examples: Ebay, Amazon Warehouse

The platforms not only synthesize information but also offer additional features such as contacting and payment.


No direct personal information leak
Less/no chance for price negotiation

Through Agency

Examples: Furnishare, Wayfair

The seller first sell the used item to an agency and the agency sell the furniture.


Superior information reliability, no concern of information leak
Additional payment for the agent

Understand users (Persona)

I built three personas and one anti persona to specify who would be target audiences of the design and who would be not. In short, my design aims at people who hope to buy relatively cheap uesd-furniture with less concern on qualities.


Understand User Workflow (Storymap)

I then analyzed processes that sellers, buyers and angency would go through using EasyFurniture.com to understand the system structure and identify steps for improvement. Each row of sticky nots represents one identity and the branced sticky notes indicate possible alternative of the step above (i.e. multiple choices, unexpected behavior). Possbile errors(in red) are marked out and mediation methods are provided (in the same color with main sticky).

Prototypes

Rapid Prototype & User Testing

In the first round of prototype, I selected five main features that would be incorporated into the design and used paper, pen, post-it and plastic sheet to quick prototype the interactions. Three students were invited to participate in the usibility testing and feedbacks were offered for modification and improvements.

Wireframe

After reflecting on the user feedback alongside with self-critisizing through QOC (Questions, Options and Criteria) analysis, the wireframes of main features were produced.
