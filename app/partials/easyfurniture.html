Problem Description

As an international student who firstly arrived at the USA, the first big challenge occured to me was not adapting to different weather, language or culture, but was the fact that there was no furniture in my apartment.

Buying a set of brand new furniture is not cost-effective particularly for people like me who regard the place as a temporary residency (i.e. short-term study/work), hence used-furniture becomes an ideal alternative. However, buying fine-quality furniture for an apartment effectively is more difficult than I expected.

The intention of designing EasyFurniture is to connect local used-furniture sellers and buyers in a more effcient way.

Concepts Forming

Competitive Analysis

My initial intention is to optimize the processes of used-furniture selling and buying. After investigating a number of used-furniture trading platforms both online and offline, I concluded four models of operation.



Traditional Trading


Examples: Local market
Investigate in-person, chance for price negotiation
Inflexibility in time and space

Information Synthesis Platform

Examples: Craglist, AABBS, online forums
Chance for price negotiation
Concerns on information reliability, personal information leak

Online Shopping Platform

Examples: Ebay, Amazon Warehouse
No direct personal information leak
Less/no chance for price negotiation

Through Agency


Examples: Furnishare, Wayfair
Superior information reliability, no concern of information leak
Additional payment for the agent

Understand users

I built three personas and one anti persona (the final one) to specify who would be target audiences of the design and who would be not. In short, my design aims at people who hope to buy relatively cheap uesd-furniture with less concern on qualities.


Understand User Workflow

To narrow down the scope, I decided to focus on the seller interface since it will be interesting consider a primary reason causing difficulty in second-hand furniture trading lies on them. Most of the current design does not encourage them to enter adequate information for selling local products.

Prototypes

Rapid Prototype & User Testing

In the first round of prototype, I selected five main features that would be incorporated into the design and used paper, pen, post-it and plastic sheet to quick prototype the interactions. Three students were invited to participate in the usibility testing and feedbacks were offered for modification and improvements.

Design Defense

While there are multiple options for one simple design, I use a design technique Questions, Options and Criteria(QOC) to reason my design.

Wireframe

After reflecting on the user feedback alongside with self-critisizing through QOC analysis, the wireframes of main features were produced.

Final Deliverable

The hi-fi prototype consists of all the interfaces that would occur through a journey of a seller. I used Sketch to produce screens and then used InVision to add interactions. See selected screens below.

Search for existing products

Information Review

Allow users to review item information collected online. Users can modify them later if necessary.

Image Modification

Provide functions such as image cropping, rotation, filtering to allow for simple and easy image editing. It is intended to improve image quality provided by sellers.

Reflection

Use visual assets wisely to communicate design rationale and decisions

The project is a course work of the Interaction Design course. It started from user needs identification, to design solutions and finally come to the interactive steps of prototype development and user testing. During the design process, I found visual assets such as personas, user journey map, QOC not only help me, as a designer, understand customer needs and painpoints but also serve as a tool assist communication with others.
